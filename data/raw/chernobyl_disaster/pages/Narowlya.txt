Narowlya (Belarusian: Нароўля, romanized: Naroŭlia; Russian: Наровля, romanized: Narovlya; Polish: Narowla) is a town in Gomel Region, in south-eastern Belarus. It serves as the administrative center of Narowlya District. As of 2025, it has a population of 8,388.
In 1986, the city experienced heavy radioactive fallout from the Chernobyl accident. Today it is located on the border of Polesie State Radioecological Reserve.
