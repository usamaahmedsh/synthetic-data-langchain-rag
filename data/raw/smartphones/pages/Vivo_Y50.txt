The Vivo Y50 (stylized as vivo Y50) is a mid-range Android smartphone developed by Vivo, officially announced in April 2020. The device aimed to provide a balance of modern features, including a large display, a versatile camera system, and a substantial battery, at an accessible price point.


== Specifications ==


=== Display ===
The Vivo Y50 features a 6.53-inch FHD+ "Ultra O Screen" with a punch-hole cutout for the front camera, contributing to a high screen-to-body ratio of approximately 90.77%. The display is an IPS LCD panel with a resolution of 2340 x 1080 pixels (FHD+) and an aspect ratio of 19.5:9, resulting in a pixel density of around 403 ppi.


=== Battery ===
The Vivo Y50 has its 5000 mAh non-removable battery, designed to provide extended usage. While the global version generally supports 15W wired charging, some markets may have seen different charging specifications.


== Camera ==


=== Main Camera ===
The rectangular camera module has a quad camera. The camera has a 13 MP, with an f/2.2 aperture and PDAF (Phase Detection Autofocus), the primary sensor for general photography, an 8 MP with an f/2.2 aperture and a 13mm focal length, offering a 120-degree field of view designed for capturing wider scenes like landscapes or group photos, a macro 2 MP, with an f/2.4 aperture is dedicated to close-up shots, allowing users to capture fine details from a very short distance, and a depth 2 MP, with an f/2.4 aperture sensor that assists in creating portrait mode photos with a blurred background (bokeh effect) by accurately measuring depth information.


=== Rear Camera ===
The Y50 features a 16 MP camera module displayed on the left, with an f/2.0 aperture.
