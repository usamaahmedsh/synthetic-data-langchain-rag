Advan is an Indonesian electronics company. This company was founded in 1999 in Jakarta. This company produces various kinds of electronic equipment. Famous products produced by Advan are smartphones, computers, tablets, and laptops.


== History ==
Advan was founded in Indonesia in September 1999 by Rudy Tirta, starting from the Indocomtech fair in Jakarta. The collaboration between Advan's Research & Development (R&D) Department and the Taiwanese brand created computer, laptop, smartphone and tablet computer products. Advan products are targeted at all groups of students, professionals and computer enthusiasts who consider the importance of performance, feature package and reasonable price.
In 2009, Advan expanded its assembly plant in Semarang, which was previously located in Taiwan, with the aim of reducing production prices.
In 2011, Advan launched several tablet with Android OS version 4.0 (Ice Cream Sandwich) and 4.2 (Jelly Bean) ranged from 7", 8,4" and 9" screen.
in 2014, Advan released Hammer, a series of low cost featured phone coded R1, R3, R5 and R7, aimed for a niche market which only need basic communication feature. 
In 2015, Advan discontinued the AIO Deskbook product. In 2017, Advan discontinued the Vanbook notebook product, while introducing mobile phone operating system IDOS based on Android, and focus on the smartphone market.
In 2019 a rumour spread, mentioning that Advan had gone bankrupt. It turned out that the company that filed for bankruptcy was PT. Advan Solusi Teknologi, a completely unrelated entity from PT. Bangga Teknologi Indonesia who owned Advan smarphone brand. The misunderstanding was caused by several media report on the bancrupty ot PT. AST (which operated in oil and exploration business) attached Advan's logo by mistake, unaware of the unrelated nature between the company 'Advan' with the brand 'Advan'.
In 2022, Nokia announced that they will launch their newest feature phone produced in Advan's manufacturing plant in Semarang.
In 2024, Advan rebranded their logo and slogan, at the same time with the launch of their newest laptop, Advan AI-Gen Ultra and a gaming console, Advan X-Play.

=== Featured Phone ===
R1X: simple candybar phone with dual SIM GSM phone with 1,7" screen, 32MB memory, 0,08MP camera, torch function and 800mAH battery.
R2: simple candybar phone with slightly upgraded feature compared to R1X, with external memory slot up to 32 GB. 
R3: simple candybar phone with more emphasize on entertaintment compared to R1X, with 2,4" screen, analog radio, analog TV receiver and VGA camera.
R5: simple flip/clamshell phone with external memory slot up to 32 GB, 2,4" main screen, analog radio, analog TV receiver and QVGA (1,3MP) camera.
R7: simple candybar phone with slightly upgraded feature compared to R1X, with 2,8" screen, up to 16GB micro SD slot and 850mAH battery.
P4: simple candybar phone with 1,8" screen, 32MB memory, 0,3MP (VGA) camera, up to 16GB micro SD slot and 2.500mAH battery.
CT1 (2018): branded as smart feature phone, this simple candybar phone was equipped with Android 4.42, 3G network, 2,4" screen, 256 MB RAM, 512 MB ROM, 2MP main camera, and 1400 mAH battery. 
HP Online (2020): branded as smart feature phone, this simple candybar phone was equipped with Kai OS (the only Kai OS manufactured in Indonesia), 4G network, 2,4" screen, 512 MB RAM, 4GB ROM, up to 32GB micro SD slot, 2MP main camera, and 0,3MP (VGA) front camera.
R6: candybar feature phone with GSM network, 2,4" screen, 32 MB RAM, 32 MB ROM, up to 32GB micro SD slot,  QVGA camera, FM radio, 1600 mAH battery, and bluetooth 2.1.
