The Antipatrid dynasty (; Ancient Greek: Ἀντιπατρίδαι) was a Dorian Greek dynasty of the ancient Greek kingdom of Macedon founded by Cassander, the son of Antipater, who declared himself King of Macedon in 305 BC. This dynasty did not last long; in 294 BC it was swiftly overthrown by the Antigonid dynasty.
Members of the Antipatrid dynasty:

Antipater, regent, not king
Cassander (305–297 BC)
Philip IV of Macedon (297 BC)
Alexander V of Macedon (297–294 BC)
Antipater I (296–294 BC)
Antipater II Etesias (279 BC)
