== Early life ==

August Bolte was born in Franklin County, Missouri in 1854 to William Henry Bolte and Wilhelmina Charlotte Haase Bolte. He married Christina Arand in 1882 and had two children. He was a lawyer and served as a probate judge from 1881 to 1894 and was the Franklin County prosecuting Attorney in 1895.


== Political career ==
August Bolte was first elected to office as Lieutenant Governor of Missouri alongside Lawrence Vest Stephens on November 3, 1896, winning with 50.56% of the vote. He took office on January 11, 1897, and during his tenure, Bolte sought the Democratic nomination for U.S. Representative from Missouri's 10th District. He won the nomination, but went on to lose against republican incumbent Richard Bartholdt in the general election on November 6, 1900.


== Later life and death ==

Upon reaching the end of his term on January 14, 1901, and following his election loss, August Bolte retired from politics. Bolte died in St. Louis, Missouri, on June 24, 1920, aged 65. He is buried at Immanuel Lutheran Cemetery in Washington, Missouri.
