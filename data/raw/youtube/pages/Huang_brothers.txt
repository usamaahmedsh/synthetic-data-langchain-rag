Cary Kaiming Huang and Michael Yiming Huang (born March 18, 1997), referred to together as the Huang twins, are a duo of American YouTubers, voice actors, and animators who co-created the indie-animated web series Battle for Dream Island and the interactive online visual tool The Scale of the Universe.


== Early life and education ==
The Huang brothers were both born on March 18, 1997 in Moraga, California. While attending school, they began using the multimedia software program Adobe Flash. Cary "started using Flash when he was 10 years old". Cary later studied computer science at Stanford University and Michael studied film at the University of California, Berkeley.


== Online projects ==


=== htwins.net ===
In 2005, the twins created the website htwins.net, where they released and hosted several Flash games, including The Scale of the Universe and its sequel The Scale of the Universe 2.


== YouTube channels ==


=== jacknjellify ===
On February 4, 2008, the twins began uploading on the YouTube channel jacknjellify. On January 1, 2010, the first episode of the animated web series Battle for Dream Island was uploaded to YouTube.


=== Abacaba ===
On June 21, 2014, Cary launched Abacaba, a YouTube channel where he uploaded data visualization videos pertaining to topics such as chess, internet memes, and the COVID-19 pandemic.


== Impact ==
The Huangs' web series Battle for Dream Island inspired a genre of similar animated web series called "object shows".
The Scale of the Universe was featured on NASA's Astronomy Picture of the Day in 2018 and inspired the Kurzgesagt app The Universe in a Nutshell, which was released in 2020.
On June 16, 2021, the International Astronomical Union gave the main-belt asteroid 1971 UD1 the name Caryhuang after Cary Huang, in part for his involvement on The Scale of the Universe.
