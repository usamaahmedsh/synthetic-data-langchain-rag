He was born in Winchester, Hampshire, and educated at Winchester College and New College, Oxford. He played first-class cricket for Oxford University from 1827 to 1832. He was made a fellow of New College in 1838. He became a Church of England priest and was rector of Stratton St Michael, Norfolk, from 1839 until his death there in 1873.
