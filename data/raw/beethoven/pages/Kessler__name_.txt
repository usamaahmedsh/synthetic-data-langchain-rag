Kessler or Keßler is a surname of German origin. It is an occupational name that means coppersmith, or more precisely, a kettle (German Kessel) maker. In alpine countries, the name derived from the definition "the one living in the basin of a valley". The surname is frequent in America among Jewish (Ashkenazi) communities.
