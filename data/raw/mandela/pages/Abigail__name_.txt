Abigail is a feminine given name. The name comes from the Biblical Hebrew: אֲבִיגַיִל / אֲבִיגָיִל ʾĂḇīḡayīl, meaning "my father's joy" (alternatively "my father is exulted" or "my father is joyful", among others). It is also a surname.
The name can be shortened to Abbey, Abby or Abbie, Abbi, Abi, or Aby, as well as Gail and Gayle, among others.


== Biblical figures ==
Abigail is the wife of King David in the Hebrew Bible's Book of Samuel, and is described as an intelligent, beautiful, loyal woman.
Abigail is the mother of Amasa, the commander-in-chief of Absalom's army (2 Samuel 17:25).
