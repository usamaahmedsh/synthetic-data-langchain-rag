This is a list of Corsica, Sardinia, and Sicily species extinct in the Holocene that covers extinctions from the Holocene epoch, a geologic epoch that began about 11,650 years before present (about 9700 BCE) and continues to the present day.
This list includes animals that have gone extinct from Corsica, Sardinia, and Sicily, three islands in the middle of the Mediterranean belonging to France and Italy. 
Many animal species have disappeared from Corsica, Sardinia, and Sicily as part of the ongoing Holocene extinction, driven by human activity.
