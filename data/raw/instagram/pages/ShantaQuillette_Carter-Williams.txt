ShantaQuillette Carter-Williams, also known as Shanta Q, is an American comedian and health advocate. She combines humor with public health education on her TikTok platform, Shanta Q.


== Early life and education ==
ShantaQuillette Carter-Williams is a distinguished professional from Dallas, Texas. She holds a Master’s degree in Business Administration with a concentration in forensic accounting, as well as a Ph.D. in the humanities. With over a decade of experience at the IRS, she has developed significant expertise in financial investigations and compliance. Her extensive knowledge and commitment to her field make her an exemplary figure in her profession.


== Career ==
After enduring a heart attack and two strokes, ShantaQuillete Carter-Williams has remarkably navigated her path to recovery through the mediums of comedy and storytelling. Her initial TikTok videos, presented under the handle "HeyShantaQ," rapidly gained popularity, captivating audiences with a sophisticated blend of humor and health insights. Currently, Shantaquillete engages an impressive following of over two million individuals on TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube, where she produces thoughtful and informative content that focuses on women’s health, aging, motherhood, and self-care. Her journey serves as a powerful inspiration to many.


== Health and advocacy work ==
ShantaQuilette Carter-Williams serves as a National Ambassador for the American Heart Association and is a valued member of the Advisory Board for the American Stroke Association. She is a dedicated advocate for health equity and actively works to raise awareness about cardiovascular diseases, particularly within women and marginalized communities. Additionally, she is the founder of "The Pink Peppermint Project," a nonprofit organization that has successfully provided over one million dollars in care packages and wellness resources to support women facing breast cancer. Her commitment to these causes profoundly impacts the lives of many individuals.


== Personal life ==
ShantaQuillette Carter-Williams lives in Dallas, Texas, with her husband Roy Williams Jr. and their children.
